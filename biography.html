Early Life

Sachin Ramesh Tendulkar was born on April 24, 1973, in Mumbai, India. From a very young age, he showed exceptional interest and talent in cricket. His training under coach Ramakant Achrekar at Shivaji Park shaped him into a future legend.

Rise to Stardom

Sachin made his debut for India at just 16 years old. Despite his age, he fearlessly faced some of the best bowlers in the world and quickly became known for his talent.

Career Achievements

  • Only player to score 100 international centuries
  • First male cricketer to score a double century in ODI
  • Holds record for most international runs (34,000+)
  • Played 200 Test matches — the highest by any cricketer
  • Won the ICC Cricket World Cup in 2011

Awards & Honours

  • Bharat Ratna (Highest civilian award of India)
  • Padma Vibhushan
  • Padma Shri
  • Arjuna Award
  • Wisden Cricketer of the Year

Retirement & Legacy

Sachin retired from international cricket in 2013 after a glorious 24-year-long career. His farewell match at Wankhede Stadium was emotional and unforgettable. Even after retirement, Sachin continues to inspire millions worldwide.
